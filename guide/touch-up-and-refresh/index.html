PMU Touch-Up and Refresh

The touch-up and refresh are two different appointments with two different purposes. Understanding both helps you plan and get the most from your PMU.

After the initial PMU session, there are three possible follow-up appointments: the required touch-up, an optional additional touch-up, and the yearly refresh. These are distinct appointments with different purposes, different timing, and different pricing. Knowing what each one is and when it applies removes a lot of uncertainty from the process.

The short version: touch-up within 4 to 8 weeks, refresh roughly once a year. Everything else is context.

The Required Touch-Up

Almost all PMU work requires two sessions to reach the final intended result. The initial session builds the foundation — shape, color, and density. During healing, skin responds individually: some areas retain pigment well, others heal lighter or patchier than intended. The touch-up is where Anna assesses exactly how the skin healed and refines anything that needs it.

The touch-up is booked between 4 and 8 weeks after the initial session. This window matters. Too early and the skin is still healing and the result cannot be accurately assessed. The window closes at 3 months: if more than 3 months have passed since the initial session, the appointment is reclassified as a refresh. The work involved at that point is comparable to a refresh, and it is priced accordingly.

The touch-up is a separate appointment priced separately from the initial session. It is not optional — it is the standard second step that completes the procedure.

The Optional Additional Touch-Up

Most clients do not need this. After the required touch-up, most clients have the result they want and nothing more is needed until their yearly refresh.

An additional touch-up is available for cases where one touch-up session was not quite enough: for example, if the skin retained pigment unevenly in one area, or if a small adjustment to density or shape is still wanted. It is performed 2 to 4 weeks after the first touch-up, once healing from that session is complete, and is priced separately.

Whether you need one is usually clear at the time of the first touch-up. Anna will advise you honestly at that appointment.

The Yearly Refresh

PMU fades gradually over time. The pigment breaks down as the skin renews itself, and factors like sun exposure, skin type, and skincare habits all affect how quickly this happens. A refresh restores the color and maintains the shape before the result fades unevenly or the tone shifts.

Anna recommends a yearly refresh for all clients. The quality of the work means some clients find their results hold well and choose to go a little longer between sessions. That is a reasonable judgment call, and one Anna can help you make at your appointments. What is worth avoiding is waiting until the result has faded significantly: at that point a refresh session requires more work to restore the color and shape than a timely one would have needed.

The refresh is available only to clients whose original procedure was performed by Anna. If your PMU was done by another artist and has faded, the right starting point is usually a photo assessment rather than a direct refresh booking. In some cases, correction or restoration work is needed before the result can simply be refreshed.

A refresh is priced separately from touch-up sessions and reflects the nature of the work: restoring and maintaining an existing result rather than building a new one.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I book my touch-up?

Between 4 and 8 weeks after the initial session, once healing is complete. Do not book it earlier — the result is still settling and cannot be accurately assessed. If more than 3 months have passed since your initial session, the appointment is treated as a refresh rather than a touch-up.

What if I wait more than 3 months to book my touch-up?

If more than 3 months have passed since your initial session, the appointment is reclassified as a refresh. The work involved is comparable to a refresh rather than a standard touch-up, and is priced accordingly.

Do I need an additional touch-up?

Most clients do not. After the required touch-up, most clients have the result they want. An additional touch-up is available for cases where one session was not quite enough. It is performed 2 to 4 weeks after the first touch-up and priced separately. Anna will advise you at the first touch-up appointment.

How often should I get a PMU refresh?

Anna recommends a yearly refresh. Some clients whose results hold particularly well go a little longer between sessions. Refreshing before the color has faded significantly keeps each session efficient and the result consistent.

Can Anna refresh PMU done by another artist?

If your PMU was done by another artist and has faded, send photos first so Anna can assess the existing work. A direct refresh is not always the right starting point. See PMU Restoration for those cases.
